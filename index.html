Angelica Martinez Ochoa

Lecturer & Critical Media Studies Researcher

Welcome to my professional portfolio.

I am Angelica Martinez Ochoa, a researcher, writer, and lecturer whose work moves across art, technology, and critical media studies to examine how digital infrastructures and global markets are reshaping the intimate geographies of care. My research explores the intersections of transnational labor markets, platform capitalism, and feminist science and technology studies, tracing how the design of digital platforms mediates motherhood, work, and social reproduction.

Through the lens of critical media theory, I study how emerging technologies, from gig-care platforms to generative AI, redefine the meaning of labor and value in contemporary life. My work situates these transformations within broader histories of welfare, globalization, and post-industrial economies, revealing how the global care economy operates as both a site of extraction and resistance. Ultimately, I am interested in how design, discourse, and international cooperation in a multipolar world might offer new imaginaries for equitable technological futures.
